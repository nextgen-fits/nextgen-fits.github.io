Design & Display: A Budget Watch That Doesn't Look Budget

The Bip 5 was a plastic watch with a TFT screen[reference:17]. The Bip 6 is a lightweight aluminum design with a vibrant AMOLED display[reference:18]. The 1.97-inch screen is bright, crisp, and colorful — with up to 2,000 nits of brightness, you can read it clearly even in direct sunlight[reference:19]. At 46mm, it's a large watch, but at just 27.9g, it's comfortable enough to wear all day and night for sleep tracking[reference:21].

Consumer Reports recently tested the Bip 6 and found that it measures health metrics very accurately, which is not something that can be said about all smartwatches[reference:23]. The watch holds a 4.4-star rating on Amazon after more than 5,200 reviews[reference:24][reference:25].

Fitness & Health Tracking: Accurate Where It Counts

The Bip 6 offers 140+ workout modes, including HYROX race tracking, smart strength training with auto-detection of 25 exercise types, and personalized AI coaching through Zepp Coach[reference:26][reference:27]. The built-in GPS supports five satellite systems for fast, reliable connection, and you can download free offline maps for turn-by-turn navigation during outdoor activities[reference:28].

One-Tap Measuring lets you check four key health indicators — heart rate, blood oxygen, stress, and breathing rate — in just 45 seconds[reference:29]. The BioTracker 6.0 sensor also supports heart rate variability (HRV) monitoring, a feature typically found on much more expensive watches[reference:30].

Battery Life: The Real 14-Day Test

Amazfit claims up to 14 days of battery life on a single charge[reference:31]. In our testing, the Bip 6 lasted about 4 to 7 days with continuous 24/7 wear, GPS tracking, and Bluetooth calls[reference:32]. Under heavier usage, the battery life increases from 5 days to 6 days[reference:33]. While it doesn't deliver the full 14 days under heavy use, the battery life is still impressive compared to the Apple Watch SE 3, which lasts only 18 hours.

The watch takes about 1.5 to 2.5 hours to fully charge from empty[reference:35]. Note that Amazfit includes the charging base but does not include a USB-C cable to reduce waste[reference:36].
