NH36 vs 4R36: What's the Difference?

Updated: July 20, 2026 · NextGen Horology Lab · 6 min read

If you've spent any time in the watch community, you've likely come across the debate: NH36 vs 4R36. Are they the same? Which one is better? Why does one cost more? Let's clear it all up.

The Short Answer

The Seiko 4R36 and NH36 movements are mechanically identical. The main difference is branding and availability. The 4R36 is used exclusively in Seiko-branded watches, while the NH36 is the unbranded version sold to third-party manufacturers. You're essentially paying a premium for the "Seiko" name on the rotor.

Both movements are manufactured by Seiko Instruments Inc. (SII) — the same factory, the same parts, the same quality control. The only difference is what's printed on the rotor.

The Core Difference: Branding vs. Market

The 4R36 and NH36 are two sides of the same coin, produced by Seiko Instruments Inc. The key is that one is for Seiko's own watches, and the other is for everyone else.

The 4R36 comes with the Seiko branding and is calibrated for their specific models. It's the movement you'll find inside the Seiko 5 Sports SRPD series, the Seiko Turtle, and many other popular Seiko automatics.

The NH36, on the other hand, is sold without branding to microbrands, modders, and hobbyists. It's the exact same movement, but without the Seiko price tag, making it the perfect base for custom builds.

Why Is There a Price Difference?

If the movements are identical, why does the 4R36 cost more? Simple: brand premium.

  • 🔹 4R36 (~$120-150) — You're paying for the "Seiko" name, factory calibration, and the guarantee that it's installed in a Seiko watch.
  • 🔹 NH36 (~$45-60) — You're paying for the movement itself. No branding, no markup, just the same reliable hardware.

Think of it like buying a car engine from the same manufacturer — one comes with the brand badge and dealer warranty, the other is a crate motor you install yourself. Both run exactly the same.

Which One Should You Choose?

📌 Choose the 4R36 if:

  • ✅ You're buying a Seiko watch — it comes with it by default
  • ✅ You need an authentic Seiko replacement for a service
  • ✅ You want factory calibration and warranty

📌 Choose the NH36 if:

  • ✅ You're building a custom watch or doing a movement swap
  • ✅ You're working with a microbrand that uses Seiko movements
  • ✅ You want the same performance for significantly less money
  • ✅ You're a modder who doesn't care about the brand on the rotor

Why the NH36 Is So Popular in the Modding Community

The NH36 is the go-to movement for watch modders for a few reasons:

  • 🔹 Cost-effective — at ~$50, it's affordable enough to experiment with
  • 🔹 Reliable — it's a Seiko movement, proven over millions of units
  • 🔹 Parts availability — hands, dials, and stems are widely available
  • 🔹 Easy to work on — it's a simple, robust design that's easy to service
  • 🔹 Compatible — fits most Seiko-compatible cases and dials

The 4R36 is essentially the same movement with a different name on the rotor. That's why modders overwhelmingly choose the NH36 — they're not paying for a brand name they're going to cover up anyway.

Our Verdict: Don't Overpay for the Name

If you're choosing between the two for a custom build or a movement swap, the NH36 is the clear winner. You get the exact same performance and reliability for a fraction of the cost.

If you're buying a Seiko watch, you'll get the 4R36 by default, and it's a fantastic movement. Just know that the premium you're paying is for the brand, not the engineering.

The bottom line: there's no wrong choice. Both are excellent movements. But if you're a modder or a hobbyist, the NH36 offers unbeatable value.
