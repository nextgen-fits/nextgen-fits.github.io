Building a Minimalist Triple-Monitor Setup Without the Cable Mess

Running dual-laptop, multi-monitor workflows without visual noise comes down to two things: real Thunderbolt bandwidth and a deliberate cable plan from the start.

A minimalist desk setup isn't really about owning fewer things — it's about making the things you do own invisible until you need them. The biggest threat to that is bandwidth-starved docking stations that force you to choose between display count and data throughput.

Why the dock matters more than the monitors

Most budget docks advertise multi-display support but quietly throttle bandwidth once you're running 4K across multiple ports. A genuine Thunderbolt 4 dock with full 40Gbps throughput avoids that compromise entirely — it's the difference between a setup that just works and one you're troubleshooting weekly.

The cable plan that actually holds up

Routing everything through a single vertical dock station before it reaches the desk surface is what keeps a multi-monitor setup from turning into a cable nest. Decide the routing before you mount anything — retrofitting cable management after the desk is built rarely looks intentional.
